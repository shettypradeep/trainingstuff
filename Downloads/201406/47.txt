Hi Gerrit,

here is the approach I have been using all the years, I think this is=20
quite common in companies:

1. Set up a Nexus instance in your company, mirroring Central and with=20
release/snapshot repos for your company.

1.1 Make sure that Nexus works correctly.

2. Let the CI server deploy SNAPSHOTs of you libraries deploy to your=20
Nexus instance frequently.

3. Refer to those SNAPSHOTs in your project POMs if you need bleeding=20
edge. This makes it easier to soak in fixed bugs in deps. If you think=20
that your lib is stable enough, release to SCM and Nexus.

4. Use the maven-versions-plugin to update your project dependencies and =

or fix to a release version.

It is perfectly fine to rely on SNAPSHOTs during development but not=20
when you perform mvn release:prepare release:perform.

Gru=DF,

Michael

PS: Upgrade your Maven version to a new one if you are able to.
